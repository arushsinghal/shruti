# Competitors

## Purpose

Track the competitive landscape without turning this vault into stale market research. Time-sensitive claims need a source link and verification date before they are used externally.

## Competitive Frame

Lipi should be compared against both software products and the real current workflow.

The main competitor is often:
- Doctor writing notes manually
- Assistant preparing prescriptions and follow-up tasks manually
- WhatsApp messages sent from personal or clinic phones
- Patient history reconstructed from memory or old files

## Competitor Categories

### India Speech And Clinical Documentation

Questions:
- Do they support Hindi, Hinglish, and Indian OPD language?
- Are they plain speech-to-text, medical dictation, or structured clinical workflow?
- Do they provide evidence-backed facts?
- Do they include doctor review and patient timeline?
- Do they support assistant/admin outputs?

Known names to verify:
- Augnito
- Other Indian medical dictation or clinic documentation tools

### US Ambient AI Scribes

Questions:
- Are they designed for US EHR workflows rather than Indian OPD?
- Do they rely on LLMs for clinical inference?
- How do they handle hallucination and provenance?
- Are they economically viable for Indian clinics?

Known names to verify:
- Abridge
- Nabla
- Ambience Healthcare
- Suki
- DeepScribe

### Practice Management And EHR

Questions:
- Do they own scheduling, billing, or records but miss voice-first consultation capture?
- Can they become a distribution partner instead of a competitor?
- Are they doctor-centric, assistant-centric, or clinic-owner-centric?

Known categories:
- Clinic management software
- E-prescription tools
- Patient engagement tools
- WhatsApp CRM tools

### Internal Workflow Substitutes

Questions:
- What does the assistant do today after each consultation?
- What is faster than Lipi today?
- What parts of the workflow doctors refuse to change?
- What is the real switching cost?

## Comparison Axes

Use these axes for research and sales positioning:
- Indian OPD fit
- Language support
- Evidence-backed extraction
- Doctor review workflow
- Patient timeline
- Follow-up memory
- Assistant/admin outputs
- Safety and audit trail
- Cost per consultation
- Setup burden
- Specialty coverage

## Lipi Hypothesis

Lipi wins if it becomes the trusted OPD memory and workflow layer, not merely the transcript generator.

The defensible wedge is:
- capture the consultation,
- prove every fact,
- get doctor approval,
- turn the approved record into clinic work.

## Research Queue

- Build a dated competitor table with verified pricing and feature claims.
- Collect screenshots or demo notes only from public or permitted sources.
- Interview doctors on what they currently use instead of Lipi.
- Track where competitors use LLMs for fact creation versus formatting.
- Track whether any competitor exposes source evidence for each clinical fact.
